What can I do to correct dents on my cheekbones that developed from the use of Hyalase? (photos
Consider bio-stimulators after appropriate time passed
Mar 5, 2023
The question is - how long ago were you injected? Remember that hyaluronidase will not only dissolve the unwanted filler, but your own native hyaluronic acid as well. Generally it takes 6-8 weeks for your tissues to heal and your native HA to regenerate. If the areas continue looking indented, consider bio-stimulators like Radiesse or Sculptra.
Would Radiesse and Renuva help smooth out nadolabial folds?
Treat a cause not a result
Mar 5, 2023
Radiesse Plus is an excellent product for facial support and tissue repositioning.
But logically 'nasolabial fold' is, in most cases, a result of tissue descent, therefore before placing either Radiesse or HA fillers, talk to your injector about creating an appropriate full lateral and medial cheek support. Unless there is a nasolabial 'crease', nasolabial fold should not be and in most case does not need to be 'filled'.
3 months after Radiesse, skin is still blue. Is this bruising or hyperpigmentation and what are
Tyndall is a known side effect of superficial filler placement
Mar 4, 2023
Tyndall - which is the proper name of the bluish discoloration - is a known adverse effect of Radiesse and most hyaluronic acid filler when placed too superficially in the skin. Skin thickness and color also plays a role. The only therapy is dissolving the filler - and yes - dissolving with hyaluronidase as soon as possible. Other therapies would be deep RF microneedling or laser.

Is it safe to conceive after Botox, Radiesse and Belotero injections?
Injectables do not affect fertility but you’ll still want to avoid taking them leading up to your pregnancy.
Feb 2, 2023
These are all valid concerns. The problem is, there has been very little testing done to date. Injectables do not affect fertility but you’ll still want to avoid taking them leading up to your pregnancy. Cosmetic injectables such as Botox are considered category C which means that the level of harm done on pregnant women and babies is unknown. Since there is no way to prove whether or not they are safe, you should avoid them while actively trying to conceive.
