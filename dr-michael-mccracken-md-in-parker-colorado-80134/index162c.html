Does a hair transplant (specifically FUE) look natural?
FUE's look natural
Mar 1, 2021
When performed well on appropriate candidates, FUE procedures look very natural. The transplanted follicles should be permanent. However, you will most likely continue to have at least some hair loss. Usually secondary procedures are to address this continued hair loss.
How many FUE hair grafts required to fill in my hairline, highlighted in my pictures?
FUE estimate
Mar 1, 2021
It would be beneficial to examine you, but by just looking at your photos, I would estimate that you would need 750-1000 grafts.
Hair Transplant or should I go with SMP?
FUE vs SMP
Mar 1, 2021
This is a difficult question to answer. First of all, it would be helpful to know your age and the history of hair loss in the males of your family. The younger you are and the more hair loss in your family, the less likely you are to benefit long term from FUE. Secondly, it is important to know your goals. The feasability of an FUE procedure will depend in part on how low of a hair line you want and the density you are looking for.
Does a beard hair transplant leave any scarring?
FUE beard to scalp transplant
Mar 1, 2021
FUE procedures are popular because they don't leave the linear scar of the FUT ("strip") procedure. However, there are small (usually about 1mm) circular scars where the grafts are taken. If the donor units are carefully selected and you have reasonable beard density, you could probably disguise the donor scars as long as you wear a beard. However, they would likely be visible if you were to shave your beard.

Sun Damage and FUE
Mar 1, 2021
It would be helpful to examine you or at least see photos. Typically, sun damage does not effect the survival of FUE hair transplants.
Diffuse Baldness at 23 years old, will I make a good hair transplant candidate at 30?
Thinning hair at age 23
Mar 1, 2021
You are doing the right thing by taking steps to slow the hair loss. Given the amount of loss that you have now, it is very difficult to predict how much loss you will have at the age of 30. One indicator may be the hair loss patterns of the men in your family (both maternal and paternal sides).
Will the hair in the FUE donor area grow back again?
Donor area hair
Mar 1, 2021
The donor area follicles are removed and transplanted to other areas of the scalp. Although follicles continue to generate new hairs as hair falls out, follicles do not regenerate. This is why it is crucial not to over-harvest the follices. I would think as the hair in the photo grows, you will not have visible bald spots in the donor area.
Is Rogaine use necessary for good hair transplant results?
Rogaine After FUE in a Female
Mar 1, 2021
Rogaine is often recommended as a compliment to hair transplants in men because it can slow the progression of hair loss around the transplanted follicles. In women, Rogaine can be used to reduce the risk of telogen effluvium ("shock loss"). Telogen effluvium is the loss of pre-existing hairs when follicles are transplanted too close to them. This is more common in women but is still rare and temporary.
So, you should still be able to have a good result if you are unable to use Rogaine.
