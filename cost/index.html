There are several techniques that are used for rejuvenating the upper and lower eyelids. Most often, the upper eyelid is approached with an incision placed in the upper eyelid crease. This skin heals very well with little or no scarring. Through this approach, a small amount of fat can be removed from the central part of the eyelid, which further contributes to a youthful appearance. The lower eyelid can be treated with one of two techniques, depending on the amount of excess skin present. In most cases, the lower eyelid is treated via incision on the inside of the eyelid, whereby fat can be directly removed with no scarring. The skin is then treated with laser resurfacing (usually CO2 laser) and this will tighten the skin during the healing process. When there is significant skin excess and laxity, a direct incision beneath the lower eyelashes can be used to both remove the fat and excise the extra skin. Lastly, if the lower eyelid is particularly loose, a resuspension of the corner of the eye (canthopexy) may be required. The range of these procedures in my practice could be anywhere from $3-8K based on the complexity and type of anesthetic used.
