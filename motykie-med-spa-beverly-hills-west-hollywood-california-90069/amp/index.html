Patient satisfaction is Dr. Motykie’s number one goal. At his West Hollywood practice, he and his staff provide exceptional patient care and results, while ensuring the safety and well-being of his patients throughout their experience. Nearly half of his cases involve revision plastic surgery, so he sees the shortcomings of other surgeons’ work first-hand and focuses on ensuring that his procedures are done right the first time.

Dr. Motykie is known for his ability to listen closely to each patient’s goals and laying out the best treatment plan to reach them, while setting realistic expectations and ensuring patients are fully informed. Dr. Motykie believes that education is key, and he empowers his patients with the knowledge they need to make a confident decision about their procedure.

Before he embarked on a medical career, Dr. Motykie was a personal trainer and fitness competitor, which led to his interest in learning about the human body and its functions. While studying biomedical engineering at the University of Illinois, he participated in projects that included the development of prosthetic limbs and artificial organs. He then earned his medical degree from Northwestern University Medical School, followed by his plastic surgery residency at the University of Texas. He was also fellowship-trained at Advanced Cosmetic Surgery in Beverly Hills, California.

Over his 16-year career, Dr. Motykie has developed the precise surgical skills and vast medical knowledge needed to be a top plastic surgeon. Staying on top of the latest innovations and most effective surgical techniques continues to be a priority.

Dr. Motykie is board certified by the American Board of Plastic Surgery Memberships. He is a member of the American Society of Plastic Surgeons and the American Society for Aesthetic Plastic Surgery, as well as a fellow of the American College of Surgeons.

Can Botox be used for under the chin? I don’t really have double chin.
Not for double chin
Aug 24, 2018
Botox relaxes the muscles and does nothing for fat deposits or excess skin. It can smooth out any wrinkles you might have around the neck area but there are other options for removing excess fat. Liposuction or non-surgical options like Kybella can help create a more sculpted look to eliminate the appearance of a double chin.
Does Botox raise your histamine level?
No effect on histamine levels
Aug 24, 2018
Botox itself cannot raise histamine levels. In fact, none of the neuromodulators used today, including Dysport or Xeomin, will have any impact on histamine levels. As long as you are not allergic to any compounds in Botox and know what to expect, you will not have to worry about problems with Botox.
