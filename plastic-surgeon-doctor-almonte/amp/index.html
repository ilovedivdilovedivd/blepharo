7 mo post-op lower facelift and the band under my chin is back and it's still numb - is this normal?
Recurrent Neck Bands
Dec 1, 2021
The recurrent neck bands are part of your platysma muscle. They can return after surgery and are frustrating to both patient and surgeon alike. As you healed from your original facelift, the neck tissues relaxed and the platysma band recurred. For now, Botox is a good approach because you’re still in the healing phase. Once your tissue has fully recovered (6-12 months post op), your surgeon may consider a second surgery to excise or suture those bands. Keep following up. All the best.

Will a Lip Lift help improve my deep Nasolabial Folds?
Deep nasolabial fold
Nov 10, 2021
Deep nasolabial folds are challenging for all plastic surgeons. Facelifts help, but in some very adherent folds, such as yours, the skin of the fold needs to be released and volume placed under the skin. Fat would be the best option. Direct excision is an alternative, but leaves a scar. Revisit your plastic surgeon and have a discussion. All the best.

Submental lump
Oct 23, 2021
This is a challenging problem, especially after a fairly routine procedure such as lipo and platysmaplasty. It’s often a combination of organized fluid and scar tissue. Surgeon went against my wishes with facial fat transfer - What can I do in the future to reverse thi
Fat Transfer
Oct 23, 2021
Fat transfer procedure must always come with proper expectations. You body will absorb around 10-15% of the injected fat in the first couple of months. So surgeons account for that by “over-injecting” fat by about 10-15%. Which means you will go through a phase when you think it’s been overdone. Just relax for now and allow yourself to heal. Follow up with your plastic surgeon and be reassured you are still in the early phase of healing. Good luck!
