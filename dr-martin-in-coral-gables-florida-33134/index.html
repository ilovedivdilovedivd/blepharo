Is it necessary to make an incision on the upper eye lid for ptosis surgery?
ptosis repair from inside the eyelid
Apr 18, 2023
We often will correct a ptosis from the underside of the lid so that you have no external incisions. In the office we put a drop in your eyes to evaluate the internal Mueller's muscle. The amount of lift you get with the drop then helps us to determine how much correction you will need. The lid is flipped over in the OR and some of this muscle is removed. Quick recovery for most patients.
Age 37 & eyelids have started to droop, am I a good candidate for eyelid surgery?
blepharoplasty
Apr 18, 2023
You do have a lot of excess skin for your age which can be genetic. You would do well with an upper eyelid blepharoplasty to remove some of the excess skin.
Would I benefit from eyelid surgery for droopy & exposed upper eyelids?
ptosis
Apr 18, 2023
You have ptosis of the upper eyelids, so you see more of the lid than you would like. You can do surgery to fix the ptosis, and this will decrease the amount of lid you see. There are also drops that you can use to elevate the lid, but you have to use these twice a day.

Everything will eventually drop again!

bleph with brow lift

Will Bleph and Fat Transfer improve my aging eyes?
peri-ocular wrinkles
Dec 21, 2021
You have fine lines and wrinkles around the eyes, and some hollowing in the lower lid. Either HA fillers, which can at times last many years, or fat can help with the volume loss. You should also consider either laser or radiofrequency or plasma to help stimulate new collagen growth to help improve the skin texture. Surgery to elevate the tail of the brow can also decrease the crow's feet.
One eyebrow is significantly much higher than the other. What can I do?

Nov 26, 2021
You need to submit a photo showing your eyes as well. One of the most common reasons for brow asymmetry is eyelid asymmetry. If you have a ptosis, or low eyelid height on one side, the brow on that side will be higher as it tries to help elevate the eyelid. This is improved by elevating the lower eyelid.
