Is it possible to have Lasik surgery and eyelid surgery at the same time?
Lasik and blepharoplasty
Apr 29, 2019
The answer depends on your situation but most surgeons will advise you don't do these procedures at the same time.  Primarily because each can cause dryness of the eye surface.  With lasik, some of the corneal nerves can be temporarily injured, reducing your motivation to blink and distribute tears over the surface of the eye. It's not worth the risk of causing a problem that could potentially be avoided by doing these at separate times, preferably at least 6 months apart.  
If I get Botox just in my elevens will it cause the rest of my forehead to wrinkle more t
Forehead after Botox
Mar 25, 2019
Hi,If you treat the glabella (the 11 lines) only, the forehead should not worsen, in fact it often gets better because the eyebrows lift slightly, eliminating the drive to raise the brows by tightening the forehead (frontalis muscle).  Some people exhibit something called muscle recruitment when the eyelid closing muscles try to take over for the glabella muscles that are not working as well.  But this doesnt impact the forehead. 
Why are my eyes lower and higher then each other?
Eye asymmetry
Mar 25, 2019
It's difficult to tell from your photo but the most common cause of eye position asymmetry is a difference in the position of the orbit which is the bone around the eye.  If there is a skeletal asymmetry (very common) the eye height will appear different as will the hollowness around the eyes. There are some treatments to disguise the asymmetry (e.g. fillers) but the treatment for skeletal asymmetry may not be appropriate for everyone.

Eye bags
Mar 25, 2019
The best remedy for your eye bags depends on the underlying cause. It could be allergy, prominent fat pads, or other issues that are best determined when you have an in-person consultation with a cosmetic eyelid specialist physician.  Based on your photo it is difficult to determine the cause. 
Do lumpy results need more dissolving or more filler?
Lumpy after under-eye filler
Mar 25, 2019
Seeing lumps after under-eye filler could be from the filler itself or the filler's tendency to attract and retain water locally. It can be difficult to disguise lumps with more filler- often the best treatment is to dissolve the existing filler and then refill after the area has recovered, sometimes several weeks later. It's possible more than one session of dissolving is required.  This is all assuming you have an HA or hyaluronic acid based filler.
