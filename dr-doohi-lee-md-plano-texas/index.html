What recommendations other than Botox/Dysport can you recommend to eliminate these eye wrinkles and crepe
Add Non-ablative Erbium and Smooth Threads
Jan 13, 2020
In addition to Botox, Dysport or Xeomin, I would add non-ablative Erbium laser (Fotona) treatments to tighten the skin. I noted a slight volume loss in your temple area and addition of a filler there would help as well. Other options include smooth threads, such as MINT Monothreads, to support the subcutaneous layer and encourage collengen production.
Botox for chin to relax it and to give it a more pronounced appearance?
Chin lift
May 17, 2018
I wouldn’t use Botox in this situation because asymmetric injection could alter your lips during speaking and/or smiling. Dense fillers such as Radiesse or Bellafill would be my first choice, or even fat transfer. I would recommend layered approach with the majority of filler in the deep layer with small amount in the subdermal layer to give you foundation and sculpted look.
I don’t want to do a surgery is there any other way to fix my eye shape?
Eye shape
May 17, 2018
Photograph would be helpful to give you a detailed answer, but aside from surgery or neuromodulators, judicious use of non-ablative lasers can help you tighten the tissue around your eye to give the “lift” look without over-exaggeration. I use the Fotona laser exclusively for these types of procedures where non-ablative Erbium is excellent for tightening the epidermis and dermis without ablation or downtime.

What facial fillers do I need? Should I do my nasolabial folds as well as my under eyes?
Filler
May 15, 2018
I would add a little filler under the eyes only, just to even out the depressed area. Your nasolabial folds are normal and I would not advise filling that area. Otherwise, you have a normal, full and youthful face of a 22 year old. Please be careful with the type and amount of filler so you don’t end up looking over-filled.
I have a slightly receding chin and was wondering if chin fillers would be a good option.
Receding chin
May 14, 2018
Yes, I think judiciously placed fillers would be a great option for you to start sculpting your chin. If you like the results, you may decide on a more “permanent” option including implants.
Should I have fillers or surgery for eye bags?
Eye bags
May 13, 2018
You have volume loss in the mid-face resulting in accentuation of the lower eye “bags.” Perhaps due to weight loss or just genetic makeup.
At your stage, I would start with fillers to add volume and sculpt the mid-face, including the areas under the eyes and gently accentuate the cheek bones. IMO, I would approach your face in a series of 2 or 3 sessions to gradually and naturally reach your goal.
