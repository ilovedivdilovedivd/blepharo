Can blepharoplasty result in hollow eyes for me?

May 19, 2020
Upper blepharoplasty can be successfully performed in patients with a prominent upper lid sulcus like yourself.  You would benefit from removal of the excess skin of the upper lid and it is important not to have any fat removed as this would make the deep sulcus even worse.  You may benefit from HA filler injections to fill out the lid sulcus as well, I do not find fat transposition in this area very successful in my hands.
Should I get a facelift or jaw implants?
Correction for lower face issues
May 19, 2020
Without photos it is difficult to give you specific advice.  In general, if the problem is with loosening and loss of support in the lower facial tissues a facelift will do the most to correct that.  If the problem is with the shape, size or contour of the jaw then implants would be indicated.  In some cases, both implants and facelift surgery together give the best result.  The place to start is a consultation with an experienced facial plastic surgeon.
What are these lumps on my upper eyelid? Can they be removed?

May 14, 2020
The lump you see is most likely a prolapsed lacrimal gland.  This means the tear gland for that eye has slipped downward and out into the upper lid.  The gland cannot be removed and a blepharoplasty alone will not correct the problem.  There is a surgical procedure to safely reposition the gland in the orbit, please see a facial plastic or oculoplastic surgeon with experience in this procedure.
