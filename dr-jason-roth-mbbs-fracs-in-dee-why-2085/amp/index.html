Can a facelift be used to treat nasiolabal folds?
A deep plane facelift can improve the appearance of nasolabial folds. Fat grafting is not needed.
May 22, 2022
Thank you for your question. Some types of facelift do not release the midface and midfacial fat and hence lead to little change in the appearance of the nasolabial fold. The deep plane facelift releases a group of ligaments between the skin and deeper structures call the "zygomatic ligaments". Once these are released it allows the midface to be repositioned. This will elevate the skin and soft tissues of the nasolabial fold. Fat grafting is not necessary.
Can a deep plane lift be performed on somebody who has silicone cheek implants?
Cheek implants before deep plane facelift is no problem
Sep 23, 2020
Cheek implants are inserted in a subperiosteal plane through a mucosal incision. A deep plane facelift is performed in a layer that is superficial and well away from cheek implants. It is very safe to have any facelift over the top of cheek implants and they will not be disturbed.
I have performed deep plane facelifts in many patients with silicone cheek implants and there has never been a problem.
Are deep plane facelifts the wrong technique if one wants a slightly unnatural pulled appearanc
Facial tension can be controlled through a deep plane facelift
Sep 23, 2020
Once the face has been released from it's retaining ligaments in a deep plane facelift the surgeon has to make a decision about how tight to suture the SMAS flap up in front of the ear. Very tight sutures will produce a pulled appearance. Sutures placed more loosely will give a more natural appearance. The tension in the face can be customised depending on the patient's preference, the amount of laxity in the face and neck and the surgeon's preference.
