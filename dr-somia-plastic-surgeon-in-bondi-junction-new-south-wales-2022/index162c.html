Why is a fat transfer recommended for doing my lower blepharoplasty? And what will happe
Fat transfer and lower blepharoplasty
Aug 24, 2019
Thanks for your question.Fat transfer does not prevent an ectropion. Fat transfer enhances the volume of your cheek and indirectly supports the lower eyelid. It is an important adjunct and if indicated, best performed at the time of surgery. The reason there is so much mixed info, is because of the simple fact that not all fat that is injected will survive. So have a discussion with your surgeon.Best

Lower blepharoplasty advice from the various opinions?
Lower blepharoplasty advice from the various opinions? (photos)
May 31, 2018
You have features suggestive of significant volume loss of your cheek bone. So the primary goal is to replace the lost volume and correct the imbalance. Because, loss of cheek bony volume results in loss of lower eyelid support. Because of this you would be considered high risk for PBLR ( post blepharoplasty lid retraction). Fillers alone ( you would need a large volume). If you are having surgery, then fat grafting would be a good idea along with a mid face suspension/liftBest wishes
