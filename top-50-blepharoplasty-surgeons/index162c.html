Blepharoplasty is an operation to remove subcutaneous fat in order to get rid of facial wrinkles in the upper or lower eyelid. Correction of age-related skin changes is performed with the help of a simple surgical procedure. As a result, the patient acquires an instant rejuvenating effect and a fresh, open mind.

What is blepharoplasty performed?

There are three types of surgery for blepharoplasty:
– Upper blepharoplasty (is an operation to remove fat in the upper eyelid);
– Lower blepharoplasty (the fatty layer is removed from the lower eyelids);
– Transconjunctival blepharoplasty (removal of the fat layer from the lower eyelids with a laser) is an innovative approach in modern plastic surgery.

As for the first two types of surgery, they are classified as traditional methods of aesthetic medicine.

How is the blepharoplasty surgery performed? Let’s consider some of its stages:

– The surgeon, with the help of a scalpel, makes an incision just below the growth of the eyelashes (if the lower eyelid is operated);
– Then subcutaneous fat, neoplasms and excess skin are removed;
– At the end of the operation doctor put the finest, inconspicuous stitches in a wound.

The procedure is similar in the area of the upper eyelid, but the incision is made in its natural skin fold.

Surgeons prefer to use local anesthesia during blepharoplasty surgery. The recovery period after blepharoplasty of the upper or lower eyelids, circular blepharoplasty is about 12 days. On day 3, the doctor removes the stitches. The following minor complications during recovery after blepharoplasty are possible:

  1. Swelling;
  2. Bruises;
  3. Increased sensitivity of the mucous corners of the eyes.

As a rule, these complications disappear after 5 days. Doctors recommend using moisturizing gels for the skin around the eyes and hypoallergenic drops to remove the syndrome of unpleasant sensations. It is forbidden to apply makeup to the face for 10 days.

If you have a thin and sensitive skin, then you should prepare for a long process of recovery after blepharoplasty. Modern aesthetic medicine offers a new way to perform surgery is a transconjunctive method to avoid a long recovery period and complications. In this case, the surgical procedure is performed using a laser.

Preparatory stage.

During preparation for surgery the doctor prescribes a survey in which finds the following facts:

  1. Whether the patient has any chronic diseases;
  2. Whether there are eye diseases;
  3. Whether there were earlier surgical interventions (including a maxillofacial plastic surgeries).

This information is necessary so that the surgeon can choose the safest method of conducting the operation. The necessary tests before blepharoplasty.

  • general blood analysis;
  • blood chemistry;
  • An analysis that determines the level of blood clotting;
  • An analysis aimed at detecting the level of sugar in the blood;
  • electrocardiogram.

Postoperative period and recovery

At the end of the operation, a special bandage is applied after blepharoplasty. The dressing has a cooling effect, which helps to prevent the formation of bruising and edema.

On the first day after the surgical intervention, the patient should remain in the hospital under the supervision of a surgeon, but the next day you can go home. The first two days of recovery from blepharoplasty should avoid contact with water, as well as excessive eye strain, such as reading, watching TV, working at the computer.

You need to perform gymnastics for the eyes to return the tone of the skin during rehabilitation after blepharoplasty. Exercises should be simple, for example:

  1. Rotation of eyeballs;
  2. Slight pressure on the eyeballs through closed eyelids;
  3. Moving the look up and down.

Doctors recommend using special antiseptic drops during recovery after blepharoplasty, then for the first three days. On the fifth day of rehabilitation it is necessary to undergo a scheduled examination, where sutures will be removed. You must comply with all the necessary prescriptions of the doctor during the recovery.

And if the rehabilitation process is successful, the seams become less noticeable after 3 weeks from the day of the operation. The final effect is estimated after 1.5 months. During this time, the edema subsides, and the incision site completely heals. It is necessary to use a special cream after blepharoplasty so that the postoperative scars are less noticeable.

The skill of the surgeon is developed exclusively by practice – tens and hundreds of operations, which on average is about 8-10 years of active surgical practice.

Specialization of plastic surgeon.

When choosing a plastic surgeon, you need to make sure, that he specializes in the operation you have chosen – blepharoplasty and that it is one of the main, priority in his practice.
