Cost of eyelid surgery

The Average Physician Fee for eyelid surgery is $2,724.00

Eyelid surgery (blepharoplasty) is a very rewarding procedure for properly selected patients. When properly performed, eyelid surgery will give a more youthful alert appearance to the patient.

The cost for this procedure varies from doctor to doctor and geographic location to geographic location. Recent statistics published by the American Society for Aesthetic Plastic Surgery report that the average physician fee for this procedure is $2,724.

This fee does not include the fee that will be charged by the facility in which you have this procedure. Additionally, if an anesthesiologist is required there will be another fee.

If the upper eyelids are operated on only or if the lower eyelids are operated on only, one would expect the fee to be close to the same for each individual procedure.

Normally, the surgeon’s fee includes all of the post-operative care associated with the procedure. When two or more procedures are performed at the same time, the overall cost is less expensive.

This is due to the fact that the first hour of surgery requires the opening of all of the instruments and other items necessary for the procedure.

Subsequent operations performed at the same time do not include this initial fee. For these reasons, upper and lower eyelid surgery performed at the same time is less expensive than it would be if these two procedures were separated and done at different times (John J. Edney, MD, Omaha Plastic Surgeon)

Eyelid Surgery Ranges Between $3,000-$3,500 in the Midwestern, US

The cost of eyelid surgery varies significantly and is dependent upon a multitude of variables. It’s important to understand these variables and how they contribute to any fee quote that you may receive.

For instance, does the fee quote include the surgeon’s fee, facility fee, and anesthesia charges or just the surgeon’s fee? Does the fee quote include local or general anesthesia? These are just a few of the questions that need to be answered before proceeding.

It’s important to make sure that your fee quote includes the surgeon’s fee, facility fee, anesthesia charges and pre and post-operative care. Generally, surgeons who operate in dedicated outpatient cosmetic surgery facilities are able to offer lower facility fees than those who operate in hospital based practices. The surgeon’s fee can vary as well depending on regional differences and levels of local competition. Anesthesia fees can vary and this may be related to the use a nurse anesthetist verses a physician anesthesiologist. Also the type of anesthesia may contribute to fee differences. General anesthesia is more expensive than local anesthesia. When all of these variables are considered, fees in the Midwestern United States for upper and lower eyelid surgery performed together range from $4000 to $5000. When either lower or upper lid eyelid surgery is performed separately the fees range from $3000 to $3500. These fees would include general anesthesia. It’s important to remember that although price is an important component of your decision, it’s not the only consideration. It’s also important to be comfortable with your surgeons experience and reputation. (Richard J. Bruneteau, MD, Omaha Plastic Surgeon)

Eyelid Surgery Costs in the Midwest, US.

As with any cosmetic surgery, the costs of blepharoplasty will differ from one surgeon to another and from one area of the country to another. Difference also exist depending on which regions require attention. Typically, if it is only the upper eyelids or only the lower eyelids, the cost will be the same.

If both the upper and lower eyelids are done together then there is a built in discount since you are paying only once for facility and for the anesthesia fees. Also, usually the surgeon will discount the fees on the secondary procedure when two or more are combined.

At our private Midwestern plastic surgical practice and in our own accredited surgical facility, the cost for either bilateral upper or bilateral lower eyelid blepharoplasty is $3100.

If upper and lower eyelids are done together then the fee is $4200. This includes the surgeon’s fee, the facility fee and anesthesia. The only out of pocket expenses that you may incur would be for any dressing supplies (ie gauze, sponges) and prescriptions.

These tend to run approximately $60. Also, sometimes insurance will cover the prescriptions even though they do not cover the surgery. (Marie E. Montag, MD, )
