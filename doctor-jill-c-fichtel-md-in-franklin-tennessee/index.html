Is it impossible in some people to get forehead Botox without lowering the brow?
Eyebrows and forehead Botox
Dec 19, 2018
Botox for the forehead affects a muscle called the Frontalis.  This muscle is largely responsible for the "resting" position of your eyebrows. Therefore, affecting the ability of the forehead to contract by injecting Botox can certainly have an effect on the resting position of the eyebrows.

Dec 19, 2018
A few reasons why Botox may no longer be working may include:- Your most recent injector didn't use enough Botox- Injection technique was different- You may need to consider Xeomin or Dysport, which work just like BotoxMake sure your injector is a board-certified Dermatologist or Plastic Surgeon.

Lower eyelids - multiple options
Dec 19, 2018
You should be evaluated in-person.  Hyaluronic acid fillers may help improve the appearance of your lower eyelids.  However, a lower eyelid blepharoplasty may be your best bet.
Has my Botox migrated?
Botox affecting the eyelids
Dec 19, 2018
If Botox is injected too low, it can sometimes affect the upper eyelids and cause them to droop.  However, it is difficult to tell if this is what's causing your problems.  This issue needs to be evaluated in-person.  I recommend you consult with a Dermatologist or Plastic Surgeon.
