I have high blood pressure. Is a tummy tuck safe for me?
Tummy tuck and blood pressure
Feb 27, 2020
It is advisable to have your blood pressure under control before surgery. This means that your blood pressure should be below 140/90 and ideally below 130/85. This will reduce your risks both during and after surgery.
How long do I need to take off work after a tummy tuck?
Tummy tuck and work
Feb 27, 2020
I usually tell my patients that 2 weeks off work is enough for desk type jobs. I have my patients refrain from any exercise or heavy lifting for 4 weeks. If your job is quite physically demanding, it would be advisable to hold off until 3-4 weeks, or go on modified duties.
What type if procedure/s would I need to get rid of extra skin?
Tummy tuck candidate
Feb 27, 2020
You are a perfect candidate for a full tummy tuck. This will not only remove the excess skin, but it will tighten the abdomen and narrow the waistline while flattening the tummy!!
Am I a candidate for a mini tummy tuck or a full tummy tuck?
Mini Tummy Tuck
Feb 27, 2020
It is difficult to tell in a photo as I need to pinch the area to see how much laxity there actually is. If while you are bent at the hips around 45 degrees and can get your belly button to pull down quite low, you need a full tummy tuck. Best thing to do is get a consultation with a plastic surgeon that you feel comfortable with.
Prone to blood clots, is getting a tummy tuck safe?
DVT and Tummy Tuck
Feb 27, 2020
This is definitely concerning and I would send you to see a hematologist for a full work up. If it all came back clear and they gave the go ahead for surgery, I would treat you with a blood thinner (injection) the morning of surgery and for 2 weeks after. Also I use TEDS stockings and SCDs for all patients. Lastly, I would limit the amount of lipo or any other procedures concurrently.
Why is an embolism more likely to happen with a Tummy Tuck?
Tummy tuck and blood clot
Feb 27, 2020
Blood clots in the veins of the calf muscles can move up to the lungs and be very dangerous. A tummy tuck has an increased risk of these and when liposuction is added or another procedure, it amplifies the risk. It’s best to reduce your risks but stopping smoking, holding estrogens, and losing weight. Your surgeon will stratify your risk and give you blood thinners if they feel it will make the surgery more safe.
Would I be a good candidate for a skin only tummy tuck?
Tummy Tuck and rectus diastasis
Feb 27, 2020
If you have a separation of the muscles, not repairing them in a Tummy Tuck would result in a less than ideal outcome. That being said, I think it would be reasonable to discuss your concerns with you surgeon and perhaps have them do an anatomic repair to prevent over tightening to maintain a natural look.
