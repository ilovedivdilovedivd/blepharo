Age 30, would lower facelift help or fat transfer help lines?
Deepening Marionette Lines
Mar 14, 2022
Yes, marionette lines deepen as you age. The nonsurgical treatments you described are appropriate treatments, they just don’t last very long. Fat transfer and/or lower facelift are more robust procedures which last longer, although not forever. Seek out a plastic surgeon who specializes in facial rejuvenation for a personal consultation and advice. All the best
Could cervical spine injury be due to lipo on neck & chin?
Pinched nerves and neck lipo
Mar 9, 2022
Thank you for the question. So sorry you’re experiencing the nerve problems. The answer is no. Neck Liposuction is removal of fat under the chin. The plastic surgeon is nowhere near the spine during lipo. Spinal changes are typically a result of degenerative disc disease, which is managed by a spine surgeon. I wish you all the best.
Fellow performing a facelift – do they have the necessary experience?
Surgery by a Fellow
Mar 9, 2022
A fellow is a fully qualified surgeon in the final years of their training. He/she has been trained by the attending surgeon to achieve the results promoted by the attending. So you’re in good hands. Should any problems arise, they collectively would be on hand to provide solutions. I wish you the best.
64 years old – what procedures would benefit me most?
What to do next
Mar 9, 2022
Thank you for the question. I recommend doing things in stages. Based on your photos I would suggest to start with a face and neck lift as well as a lip lift. Those procedures will go a long way to restoring a more youthful look. You can then decide if skin resurfacing and treating your eyelids would further enhance your outcome. I wish you all the best!
Will a Lip Lift help improve my deep Nasolabial Folds?
Deep nasolabial fold
Nov 10, 2021
Deep nasolabial folds are challenging for all plastic surgeons. Facelifts help, but in some very adherent folds, such as yours, the skin of the fold needs to be released and volume placed under the skin. Fat would be the best option. Direct excision is an alternative, but leaves a scar. Revisit your plastic surgeon and have a discussion. All the best.
