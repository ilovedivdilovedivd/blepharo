Statement: Dr. Timothy Cooper is a leading specialist plastic and reconstructive surgeon in Nedlands, Western Australia, with over 25 years in practice. He is the founder of the Rodin Clinic, named for the famous French sculptor in honor of his passion for sculpting and painting. There, Dr. Cooper’s patients can choose from a wide range of surgical and nonsurgical aesthetic procedures. He specializes in rhinoplasty, facelifts, and breast surgery, including breast augmentations, breast lifts, and breast reductions.

Dr. Cooper earned his medical degree at the University of Tasmania. He went on to receive advanced training in plastic and reconstructive surgery at the Cork University Hospital in Ireland, the University of Pittsburgh, and the Davies Medical Center in San Francisco. He completed his training at the Prince of Wales and Sydney Children’s Hospitals in Sydney.

He also worked for several years for the Royal Flying Doctor Service, delivering medical care in rural and remote Australia. An instrument-rated pilot, Dr. Cooper continues to provide specialist surgical services to the Mid West region of Western Australia.

Dr. Cooper is board certified in surgery and plastic surgery by the Medical Board of Australia, in plastic and reconstructive surgery by the Royal Australasian College of Surgeons, and in plastic surgery by the Royal College of Surgeons of England.

He is a member of the Australian Society of Plastic Surgeons, the Australasian Society of Aesthetic Plastic Surgeons, and the International Society of Aesthetic Plastic Surgery, as well as a corresponding member of the American Society of Plastic and Reconstructive Surgeons.

Dr. Cooper is widely published in peer-reviewed articles and regularly presents at both national and international surgical meetings. As a former supervisor of postgraduate training, Dr. Cooper has been responsible for training and mentoring over 40 plastic surgeons in Australia. In recognition of his plastic surgery service and volunteer work in Bali, Indonesia, he has been awarded several prestigious honors, including an Order of Australia AM.

Hi,
Let me start off by stating fillers only have a limited role in augmenting defects of the nose. You require a rhinoplasty and my preference would be to perform an open rhinoplasty.
