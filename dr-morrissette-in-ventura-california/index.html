How does a 9mm advancement affect my facial harmony?
sliding genioplasty
Dec 3, 2023
You have a very recessive chin based on the lateral ceph. I would recommend an advancement genioplasty of 9-1- mm. That is the extent of advancement that can be safely performed while maintaining bone contact. It does not appear that you will need a bone graft and the vertical height looks within the normal range.
2 year old chin implant doesn’t feel stable – should I be concerned?
chin implant and fixation
Nov 22, 2023
It is possible that the implant has migrated due to lack of screw fixation so I would get a CT scan to evaluate the implant position. Once an implant has been in for a few months it will become encapsulated and therefore should not move unless it has migrated off of the bone. It can easily be removed and replaced or even repositioned.
Am I a good candidate for sliding genioplasty? How many mm can my chin be advanced? (
sliding genioplasty
Nov 20, 2023
Yes, the sliding genioplasty would be best in your case. The chin can be advanced 9-10 mm. In some cases, I place a chin implant later if more projection is needed (cases that need 15 mm). You do not need to undergo corrective jaw surgery if your bite is fine. The sliding genioplasty is performed in the office with sedation anesthesia and takes about 45 minutes. There is no change in your bite or chewing. Most patients return to work in 3-5 days.
Can genioplasty help?
deep labiomental fold
Nov 17, 2023
The treatment for a deep labiomental fold is to vertically lengthen the chin. Your lower facial height is actually short which is why you have a deep labiomental fold. The sliding genioplasty would lengthen your chin and diminish the depth of the labiomental fold but it will not impact your facial balance in a negative way. The position of your chin is fine and no further advancement is needed. solely, vertical lengtheing.
Sliding genioplasty, jaw surgery or chin implant to fix my profile? What will happen
sliding genioplasty
Nov 17, 2023
In the one photo, it appears that you are deficient vertically and horizontally. You have a deep mentolabial fold which tells me that you need vertical lengthening. A sliding genioplasty can provide both lengthening and advancement with one operation. Vertical lengthening of the chin cannot be adequately accomplished with a chin implant. It tends to deepen an already deep fold. The procedure should improve the balance of your lower face.
