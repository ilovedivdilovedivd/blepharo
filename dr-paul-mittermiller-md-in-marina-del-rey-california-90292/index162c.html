Has bone erosion been observed in the years after a Sliding Genioplasty?
Sliding genioplasty
Jan 11, 2022
Bone erosion does not typically occur with a sliding genioplasty. It occurs most commonly from chin implants such as those made from silicone. On the contrary, after a sliding genioplasty, bone forms in and around the cut(s), which helps with smoothening the contours.
Is shaving the tip of the chin safe?
Shave genioplasty
Jan 11, 2022
Shaving the chin can be safe if there is a moderate decrease in the size of the chin. It can be accessed through the mouth and would require resuspending the muscles. When performed properly, this is a safe procedure. If you need a significant decrease in the size of the chin, it may be safer to perform an osseous genioplasty. The correct procedure will also depend on which dimensions you want to reduce the chin (vertical, horizontal, etc.).
Raising / improving surgical scar on cheek – options?
Scar tethering
Jan 11, 2022
If the scar is tethered to deeper tissue, a surgeon could attempt to release the tethering using a needle and fat graft the area. If there is no tethering, you could simply try a small amount of fat grafting or filler to the area.
During scar revision, how can you remove tension and keep the scar from stretching?
Scar management
Jan 11, 2022
Scars over the shoulders are difficult to prevent from widening over time because there is usually a large amount of movement and tension in the area. Depending on how the scar appears, you could consider re-excising the scar and then trying to offload tension on the area afterwards with taping or silicone strips. You may still get widening over time though.
