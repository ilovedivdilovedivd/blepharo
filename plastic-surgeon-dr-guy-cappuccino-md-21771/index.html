Statement: Dr. Guy Cappuccino is a board-certified plastic surgeon serving Montgomery, Howard, Carroll, and Frederick counties in Mt. Airy, Maryland. He specializes in breast enhancement, tummy tucks, and full mommy makeovers, as well as hair restoration, facelifts, eyelid surgery, and body contouring. His patients know him to be a highly compassionate, thoughtful doctor who delivers artistic, natural-looking results and prides himself on individualized attention.

“Great outcomes in plastic surgery begin with thorough initial consultations,” he says. “I really want to get to know my patients and what their goals and expectations are for surgery. I consider my first responsibility to be a patient educator and advocate. I’m quick to tell a patient when they don’t need surgery as well.” His entire office staff is dedicated to making the experience as pleasant as possible, from the initial consultation through post-op visits.

Dr. Cappuccino graduated summa cum laude with a bachelor’s degree in biochemistry from the honors college at the State University of New York at Buffalo, and went on to earn his medical degree from the University of Buffalo School of Medicine and Biomedical Sciences. In addition to training in general surgery at New Jersey’s busiest level-one trauma center, University Hospital in Newark, he completed a residency in plastic and reconstructive surgery at the University of Medicine and Dentistry of New Jersey. He is board-certified by the American Board of Plastic Surgery.

In addition to his work serving patients of the Washington and Baltimore metropolitan areas at his Mt. Airy practice, Dr. Cappuccino is very active in the medical community. He was recently features on Good Morning America for his work during the COVID-19 pandemic. He has authored multiple papers that have been published in peer-reviewed journals and textbooks on the topics of breast augmentation, hand injuries, and more. Dr. Cappuccino is a frequent guest lecturer at industry-wide conferences around the country, and is a member of multiple professional organizations, including the Maryland Society of Plastic Surgeons, the American Society of Plastic Surgeons, and the John Stage Davis Society of Plastic Surgeons.

Dr. Cappuccino also traveled on medical missions to Africa, offering his skills in cosmetic and reconstructive surgery to children in Mauritius with congenital deformities and burns.

In his spare time, Dr. Cappuccino is active as a pianist at his local church. He’s also a licensed, instrument-rated commercial pilot, a certified flight instructor, and an aviation medical examiner.
